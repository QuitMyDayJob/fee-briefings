Moderator Role Briefing

What you're signing up for. Read this before you throw your cap in. I'd rather you read this and decide it's not for you than say yes and burn out in three weeks. No hard feelings either way.

What we're building

The community spans two pillars. They behave differently, and the team as a whole needs to cover both well — but no single mod needs to be expert in both.

// PILLAR 01
Competitive Shooters
Fast, loud, clip-heavy, mechanically driven. Chat is reactive. Short-form is the engine.
// PILLAR 02
Single-Player Games
Slower, narrative, conversational. Chat is discussion-driven. Long-form retains.

We grow when we consistently put great content in front of new people across long-form, mid-form, and short-form channels — and we retain those people because the community feels like a place worth staying. Moderators are the people who make that engine run. You're not just keeping chat clean. You're a core part of how this thing grows.

What we're optimizing for

Every responsibility below ladders up to one of these. If you ever wonder why a task matters, check it against this list.

01
Organization
So we stop dropping balls and I stop being a bottleneck.
02
Community Engagement
So the people who find us stay and go deeper.
03
Distribution
Long-form, mid-form, short-form. So new people find us in the first place.

Distribution is the growth lever. Engagement is the retention lever. Organization is what makes both possible.

Who we're looking for

Before duties, before quotas — these are the non-negotiable traits. If these don't describe you, the rest of this document won't matter, because you won't enjoy the role and you won't perform well in it.

// CORE TRAIT 01
Active Presence, Not Lurking
We're a small community. Every moderator is load-bearing. If your idea of being a mod is having the title and lurking quietly in chat, this isn't the role for you. We may add a quieter mod tier as we grow — right now, presence and engagement are pillars, not nice-to-haves.
// CORE TRAIT 02
Team Player, Unity First
We have each other's backs. We don't deflect or point fingers when things go sideways. We figure it out together, fix it, and move forward. If your instinct under pressure is to assign blame or distance yourself, the team dynamic we're building won't work for you.
// CORE TRAIT 03
Energy Mirror
You match and amplify the energy I bring — fun, upbeat, friendly, positive. When the vibe starts shifting toward toxic or contentious, you use your influence to redirect it before it escalates. You handle timeouts and bans with tact, not ego.
// CORE TRAIT 04
Direct & Coachable
You can take honest feedback without taking it personally, and you give the same back to me. The dynamic only works if we can both speak plainly. If you'd rather be told things gently and indirectly, this isn't the right fit.

Team Structure

The mod team is split into two tiers. They share the same baseline expectations (presence, chat engagement, team values) — the difference is in their focus area and clip output.

Content Moderator
// TIER A
5
Clip Submissions / Week
Leans into the distribution engine. Sharp eye for clip-worthy moments across both pillars. Higher contribution to the short-form pipeline.
Community Moderator
// TIER B
2
Clip Submissions / Week
Leans into chat hosting, community vibe, event support, and prediction/poll idea pipeline. Lower clip floor, higher community-side engagement.
Both tiers must hit quality standards. Clip quotas are floors, not ceilings, and any clip submitted must meet the quality bar — it has to be something we'd actually post. Hitting the number with weak submissions doesn't count.

Universal Expectations

These apply to everyone, both tiers. If you can't see yourself doing these consistently, this isn't the right role for you right now — and that's okay.

EXP-01 In-Stream Presence

A minimum of 2 hours of active presence per stream, assuming a full 6-hour session. Life happens and that's understood — but this is the standard, not a stretch goal. Across most streams, most of the time, you're there.

"Active presence" means engaged in chat, not just having the tab open in the background.

EXP-02 Chat Engagement & Energy Management

This is the most important duty after presence itself. You're not just typing in chat — you're shaping the vibe.

// What this looks like in practice
  • Mirror and amplify the energy I'm giving off — fun, upbeat, friendly, positive
  • Greet new viewers and make returning viewers feel seen
  • Keep conversation alive in slow moments without forcing it
  • Spot tension between community members early and redirect before it escalates
  • Step in on toxicity, negativity, or contentious arguments with tact, not ego
  • Issue timeouts and bans cleanly — no public showdowns, no power-tripping

The skill being built here is emotional read. Knowing when a chat is about to tip negative and intervening before it does is the highest-leverage thing a moderator can do.

EXP-03 Weekly Clip Submissions

Content Mods: 5 per week. Community Mods: 2 per week. Both must meet the quality bar.

You're not editing these — that's handled on the production side. Your job is to spot the moment and submit it in a clean format so the editor can turn it around fast.

// Submission Format
  • Timestamp — start and end
  • Stream / VOD link
  • One-line context — why this clip works
  • Suggested format — vertical short, horizontal mid, or both
  • Optional — suggested hook or title angle

The skill we're building here is judgment, not editing. Spotting what will actually perform is the hard part. We'll talk through what's landing and what isn't on a regular cadence so you sharpen the eye over time.

EXP-04 Predictions & Polls Idea Pipeline

I'll be running predictions and polls in-stream myself — that needs to stay tied to live timing and my read of the moment. What mods own is the idea pipeline.

The team will maintain a shared document where prediction/poll ideas live, along with possible answer sets and any context I need. Your job is to keep that document fresh and useful so I can pull from it quickly mid-stream without having to think it up cold.

You're welcome to participate in predictions and polls when it makes sense — but the contribution we're tracking is whether the idea pipeline stays full and high-quality.

EXP-05 Rotating Outside-Stream Assignments

A rotating duty roster covering things like: cross-platform posting schedule, community channel cleanup, event prep, sponsor coordination when relevant, content calendar upkeep. Rotations keep anyone from getting stuck on the same task forever.

EXP-06 Event Planning & Communications

Tournaments and squad nights for the shooter side. Watch-alongs, "what's next" community votes, and milestone events for single-player. Plus the comms wrapped around them — announcements, cross-channel posts, hype cycles. Each mod will contribute to planning and running at least one event per month.

How we'll work together

// Weekly Check-In

Short sync each week so we're aligned. Not a status meeting — more of a "what's working, what's stuck, what's coming." This will primarily be run through the Lead Moderator.

// Direct Feedback

I'll be more direct than I've been before. If something's off, I'll say so. I'll expect the same from you back to me.

// Clear Ownership

Every recurring task has one owner. No "I thought you were doing it" moments.

// Specialization Is Okay

You don't have to be equally great at every pillar or every duty. The tier structure already builds in some specialization — beyond that, lean into your strengths. The baseline expectations apply to everyone, but how you spend your discretionary energy can match what you're best at.

What you can expect from me

Fair is fair. If I'm asking more from you, here's what you should expect from me in return.

01 //
Clear expectations, in writing. You'll never be guessing what "good" looks like.
02 //
Real feedback, not vague nods. You'll know where you stand.
03 //
Production support. You flag the clips, the editing happens on the production side. You're not expected to be a content creator on top of being a moderator.
04 //
The same energy back. I'll show up positive, engaged, and present for the team — not just expect it one-way.
05 //
Respect for your time. If I'm asking for hours, I won't waste them with disorganization on my end.
06 //
A real seat at the table. Your input on direction, content, and community decisions actually shapes what we do.
07 //
Honesty when something isn't working — on either side, with enough runway to fix it before it becomes a bigger problem.

How to know if this is for you

Some honest questions to sit with. If most answers are yes, I'd love to have you. If a few are no, that's real information and I appreciate you taking it seriously.

Can I commit to ~2 active hours per stream, most streams, most of the time?
Am I genuinely into the gaming side — shooters, single-player, or both?
Do I bring positive energy to chat naturally, and can I help shift the vibe when it starts to tip?
Can I handle issuing a timeout or ban without making it a public power move?
Do I want to actively grow this community as part of a team, not lurk with a title?
When something goes wrong, is my first instinct to fix it together — not figure out whose fault it was?
Can I watch a stream and spot moments worth clipping, even if I'm not the one editing them?
Am I okay with direct feedback when I miss the mark?
Do I have the bandwidth in my actual life to take this on right now?
